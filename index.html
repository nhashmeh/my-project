Nadim Hashmeh

I am a planetary science graduate student at Tulane University. My primary research interests involve using ground-based and remote sensing techniques to spatially and quantitatively analyze the creation, modification, and composition of various planetary terrain. My current project involves utilizing radar sounding data from ongoing Mars orbiter missions in order to address questions regarding the nature of subsurface detections (and lack thereof) within the south polar layered deposits (SPLD). Understanding the radar behavior of the SPLD is part of a larger effort to intepret the paleoclimate record stored within the stratigraphy of the ice.
